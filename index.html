Current position

I am a graduate student at Princeton University. My thesis adviser is Frans Pretorius.

Research Interests

The main goal of most of my research is directed towards testing General Relativity (GR) in the strong field, dynamical regime. I am interested in using gravitational waves to look for dark matter/deviations from the Standard Model of particle physics as well. I have worked on analyzing the mathematical consistency of modified gravity theories used in tests of GR and invoked in early universe cosmology. I plan on expanding my work on modified gravity to modeling of astrophysically realistic solutions that include modifications of GR, for use in gravitational wave data analysis. Recently, I have begun working on understanding the regime of validity of linear black hole perturbation theory to understand the ringdown of a black hole formed after the merger. I have mostly used techniques in numerical relativity and perturbation theory.
