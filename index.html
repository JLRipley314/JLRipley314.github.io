Research interests

My research focuses on developing (numerical) methods that can be used to test General Relativity in black hole and cosmological spacetimes. I frequently use the tools of numerical relativity and gravitational perturbation theory.

Education

In 2020, I obtained my PhD from the Department of Physics, Princeton University, under the supervision of Frans Pretorius. A pdf of my thesis, General Relativity and its classical modification in gravitational collapse, can be found here.
