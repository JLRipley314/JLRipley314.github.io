Current position

I am a postdoctoral research associate at the Department of Applied Math and Theoretical Physics at the University of Cambridge.

Education

I obtained my PhD from the Department of Physics, Princeton University. My adviser was Frans Pretorius. My thesis: pdf.

Research Interests

  • The leading nonlinear dynamics of gravitational waves around black holes, for use in gravitational wave source modeling.
  • The dynamics of modified gravity theories, for use in tests of General Relativity and modeling the early universe.
For my research I mostly use the tools of numerical relativity and perturbation theory.

While there are many great resources on C/C++ and Python for scientific computing, it can be harder to find good resources on modern Fortran (i.e. Fortran 90,95, and especially 2003 and 2018). I've found the below websites useful:
  • fortran-lang.org
  • modern-fortran blog
  • fortran2018-examples (github repository)
