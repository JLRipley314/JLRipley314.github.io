Research Interests

The main goal of most of my research is directed towards testing General Relativity (GR) in the strong field, dynamical regime. I am interested in using gravitational waves to look for dark matter/deviations from the Standard Model of particle physics as well. I have worked on analyzing the mathematical consistency of modified gravity theories used in tests of GR and invoked in early universe cosmology. I have plans to solve modified gravity theories in (relatively) more astrophysically realistic setups, for use in gravitational wave data analysis (and potentially for use in cosmology as well). Recently, I have begun working on understanding the regime of validity of linear black hole perturbation theory to understand the ringdown of a black hole formed after the merger of two smaller black holes. I have mostly used techniques in numerical relativity and perturbation theory.
